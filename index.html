About me.

Arguably the most ubiquitous phenomenon in the living world, sex is a driving force in plant evolution. While most angiosperms reproduce by production of hermaphroditic flowers with both male and female organs, there exists a small fraction of plants that reproduce through dioecy - the condition of having separate male and female individuals. I work primarily in Asparagus, where dioecy is mediated by the presence of a recently evolved, active sex chromosome pair.
